CBRN Protective Suit / Type C

Active Carbon Protective Suit in Compliance with NATO Standards

CBRN Protective Suits are high-performance personal protective equipment designed to provide superior protection against Chemical, Biological, Radiological, and Nuclear (CBRN) warfare agents. Our products are manufactured in accordance with NATO AEP-38 standards to ensure maximum safety and comfort under demanding operational conditions.

Thanks to its advanced material structure and ergonomic design, the suits maintain a high level of protection even during extended periods of use. Equipped with active carbon technology, the suits effectively isolate the wearer from hazardous substances while enhancing mobility and user comfort through their air-permeable and breathable construction.

  • Spherical active carbon technology
  • Long-lasting and reliable protection
  • High mobility and superior user comfort
  • Manufacturing in compliance with international standards
  • Model options suitable for different mission requirements

Product Features

  • Protection Type: Type C – air-permeable structure
  • Configuration: Complete set compatible with boot covers, gloves, and mask
  • Adsorbents: Spherical active carbon technology
  • Hood: Design compatible with all types of CBRN masks
  • Protection Duration: Effective protection for more than 24 hours)
  • Model Options: One-piece coverall and jacket-trousers configurations
  • Design: Zippered construction at sleeves and leg cuffs
  • Sealing: Enhanced high-level sealing measures at sleeves and leg cuffs
  • Fabric Properties: Water- and oil-repellent outer fabric
  • Size Range: XS – XL (5 different size options)
  • Washing Resistance: Washed in accordance with ISO 6330, Table B.1 – Procedure 4N. Drying is performed using ISO 6330, Procedure E (press ironing) until the product returns to its original pre-wash weight.

Areas of Use

  • Military units and defense industry applications
  • Special operations against CBRN threats
  • Emergency response and civil defense teams
  • Industrial environments with chemical risk exposure
  • Disaster response and crisis management scenarios
